Persistent and anti-Persistent Motion in Bounded and Unbounded Space: Resolution of the First-Passage Problem

D. Marris and L. Giuggioli

Here we present an exact analytic framework to quantify search processes with persistence in the motion. The theory is general and applicable in arbitrary dimension, with any boundary condition. This may find application in active matter, mathematical biology and movement ecology.

In Review arXiv 2404.13360 (2024)

Multi-target search in bounded and heterogeneous environments: a lattice random walk perspective

L. Giuggioli, S. Sarvaharman, D. Das, D. Marris and T. Kay

Here we present a review.

To appear in The Target Problem (Eds. D. S. Grebenkov, R. Metzler, G. Oshanin), Springer Nature, (2024) arXiv 2404.13360

Exact spatiotemporal dynamics of lattice random walks in hexagonal and honeycomb domains

D. Marris, S. Sarvaharman and L. Giuggioli

In this work we develop a general theory to desbribe exactly diffusive processes in hexagonal geometries. We consider two lattices, the hexagonal and the honeycomb. This may find application in a variety of fields, from exciton transport on the surface of carbon nanotubes, to animal foraging theory.

Phys. Rev. E (2023)
